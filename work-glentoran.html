The situation

A great club. A digital footprint that hadn't kept up with it.

Glentoran is one of Ireland's most decorated football clubs. Twenty-three league titles, a rebuilt stadium, a social partnership doing genuine community work across East Belfast. But their digital presence had grown without direction, and it showed.

Fans looking for tickets ended up in the same navigation as commercial sponsors. Academy parents were buried under news articles. The mobile experience — where most of their audience lives — was an afterthought. GlensTV, their own streaming platform, was barely discoverable. Merchandise was sold through a system that had no connection to the physical till. There was no coherent email programme. PR was ad hoc. The club deserved better.

"The brief: give the club a digital ecosystem that reflects what Glentoran actually is — not just a football team, but a community institution with 140 years of story."

Why we were brought in

A club running at full pace — with infrastructure that couldn't keep up.

Glentoran isn't a small club with a simple digital problem. It's a 140-year-old institution with a first team, a youth academy, a social partnership programme, a physical superstore, a streaming platform, commercial partners, and a supporter base that expects more every season.

The problems weren't caused by neglect — they were caused by growth. Each part of the club had patched together its own systems and processes over time. Nobody had ever stepped back to look at the whole picture.

That was the first thing we did.

Brand inconsistency

The club's visual identity hadn't evolved as the club had. Digital, print, stadium and merchandise all pulled in different directions.

A website showing its age

51% of fans were on mobile. The site wasn't. Navigation mixed four completely different audiences into one structure with no logic.

Retail running on spreadsheets

No online store worthy of the brand. Physical till and online stock had no connection. Reconciliation was a Monday morning manual job every single week.

No EPOS, no QR, no reporting

No EPOS integration, no QR codes on products, no automated finance or admin reports. The backroom team was spending hours on data entry that should have been automatic.

Email and marketing were ad hoc

No email programme. No Mailchimp setup. No analytics to measure what worked. Commercial partners had no data to justify their investment.

PR was reactive, not strategic

No media relationships maintained. No crisis communications plan. Stories that deserved coverage went untold. Announcements landed without context or preparation.

The response

One partner. Six workstreams. Everything connected.

Most clubs split this work across three or four agencies. Nothing joins up. We took the whole brief. Each workstream was designed to work with the others — because that's the only way the results compound.

01

Brand extension

The club had grown. The brand hadn't kept pace — inconsistent across digital, print, stadium signage and kit.

We evolved the identity without erasing it. Updated typography, a disciplined colour system, and digital-first brand guidelines that brought every surface into alignment.

02

Website rebuild

The site was dated, hard to navigate on mobile, and treated four completely different audiences — fans, commercial partners, academy families, community participants — as one.

Ground-up rebuild, mobile-first. Separate user journeys for each audience. GlensTV elevated from buried sub-page to a front-and-centre fan benefit. Built on a CMS the club operates without us.

03

Glentoran Superstore

No online store that reflected the club. Kit and merchandise had nowhere worth sending people. Commercial partners had nothing to point to.

A brand-new Shopify superstore designed and built from scratch — glentoransuperstore.com. Kit launches, merchandise drops, and matchday essentials, in an experience the club is proud to promote.

04

EPOS, stock & reporting

Online and physical till were completely disconnected. Stock reconciliation was manual. No QR codes on products. Finance and admin reports took hours to assemble each week.

Full EPOS integration — Shopify unified with the physical till in real time. QR codes on every product linking directly to the online store. Automated weekly finance and admin reports. Manual reconciliation: gone.

05

Email, analytics & screens

No email programme. No web analytics. No Mailchimp setup. No visibility into what was working — or evidence to share with commercial partners.

Full Mailchimp programme with segmented audiences — fans, academy parents, partners, charity supporters, each receiving their own communications. GA4 analytics, Mailchimp reporting dashboards, and quarterly partner data packs. In-stadium screens covered too: matchday graphics, squad news, sponsor slots.

06

PR, marketing & crisis comms

PR was entirely reactive. No maintained media relationships, no crisis communications protocol, and no proactive story development. The club's community work was particularly under-told.

Retained PR service — proactive story development, media relations, commercial announcement planning, signing coverage, community and charity programme stories. Full crisis communications protocol. Coverage in national and regional print, broadcast, and digital.

"Outside of putting eleven players on the pitch, there's very little Glentoran faces as a business that Astutely doesn't have a hand in."

12 months and counting

Built on Shopify
The superstore

A proper retail experience. Online and in the ground.

The old merch setup wasn't built for a club at this scale. The online store was disconnected from the matchday till, inventory was managed manually, and kit launches had no proper destination.

We built glentoransuperstore.com on Shopify — designed to match the club's brand identity, structured around kit and merchandise collections, and integrated with the physical till system so stock moves correctly across both channels. Kit drops now have a proper moment: email campaign, social content, digital screens, and a store page that does the product justice.

Visit the superstore →
Unified commerce

Online, till, and stock — one system.

Before we got involved, the physical matchday till had no connection to the online store. Kit would sell out online while stock sat on the shelves, or vice versa. Staff were manually reconciling orders every Monday morning.

We integrated the Shopify backend with the club's physical iPad-based POS — unifying online and in-store inventory in real time. Kit launches now flow from a single product source across digital screens, email, the website, and the till simultaneously.

PR & Community

The club is more than football. The communications had to reflect that.

Glentoran's social partnership programme does real, unglamorous community work in East Belfast — schools outreach, mental health support, youth programmes. Our PR brief was to make sure that story gets told, consistently, in the right places.

Commercial partnership announcements, kit launches, match milestones, charity campaigns, player signings — every moment now has a communications plan behind it. Media relationships built, stories placed, coverage tracked.

Email & digital screens

Every channel. One consistent club voice.

Matchday email previews. Kit launch campaigns. Player signing announcements. Academy intake communications. Charity appeals.

Each audience — first-team fans, academy parents, commercial partners, charity donors — receives communications designed specifically for them. In-stadium digital screens carry the same visual language: matchday graphics, squad numbers, sponsor slots, and live score formats that make The Oval feel like the professional environment it is.
