The Situation

Growing fast into a market with no ceiling — and no map.

R Kings had the prizes. They had the audience appetite. But the UK and Ireland's competition market is noisy, short-term and built on low prices rather than real brand. The question wasn't whether R Kings could compete — it was whether they could build something that lasted.

Behind the scenes, scale was exposing cracks. Peak traffic during live draws was pushing checkout to breaking point. Email campaigns were generic. Photography had no production standard. Security protocols didn't exist. Each problem individually was manageable — together, they were a ceiling.

We came in as technical and brand consultants, and stayed. This is the story of what it takes to build a competition platform that actually holds together when thirty thousand people enter at once.

Five challenges

The problems that kept stacking up.

When you're growing fast, the cracks don't show one at a time. They all appear together — and usually at the worst possible moment.

  • A brand indistinguishable from the competition. Slick prizes, but the visual identity and tone of voice didn't justify the premium positioning or inspire long-term loyalty.

  • A platform that buckled under peak load. Draw windows brought thousands of simultaneous transactions. Checkout errors, slow pages, and failed payments during the moments that mattered most.

  • Email and SMS that ignored how competitions actually work. Generic basket abandonment at X hours made no sense when the real urgency was the draw end time — 2 hours to go is everything; 6 hours doesn't matter.

  • No security posture. No bug bounty programme, no defined incident response protocol, no structured approach to responsible disclosure or active threat monitoring at scale.

  • Prize content with no production standard. Different phones, different locations, different lighting — no brief, no consistency, no guide for what a Kings-quality shoot actually looked like.

What we did

Four workstreams.
One platform that held together.

Strategy & Brand Positioning

The competition market races to the bottom on price. R Kings' brand wasn't giving customers a reason to pay more, come back, or trust the draw.

Led a full strategic rebrand — market positioning, audience segmentation, tone of voice, and a complete Digital Experience Guide that set the standard for every touchpoint from the website to the prize shoot floor.

Platform Engineering at Scale

The Laravel-built platform had no autoscaling. Simultaneous peak transactions during draws caused checkout failures. No PCI-DSS governance, no security protocol, no defined response to breach or disclosure.

Rebuilt the architecture with autoscaling infrastructure, multi-gateway PCI-DSS compliant payments (PayPal, Visa, Mastercard, Apple Pay, Google Pay), a formal bug bounty programme and a documented hack response protocol for the team.

Email & SMS Automation

Email triggers were based on time-in-basket. In a competition business, that's the wrong signal entirely — the draw end time is the urgency. Campaigns weren't segmented, weren't personalised, and weren't driving re-entry.

Built a full Klaviyo system with 40+ automated flows. Draw end-time triggers at 72h, 24h, and 2h to close. Segmented journeys for new users, re-engagers, and winners. SMS alerts for draw day. Every flow mapped, tested, and optimised against open and conversion rates.

Videography & Content Production

Prize shoots were inconsistent — different equipment, locations, and quality depending on who turned up. Content wasn't cut for platform, meaning one shoot rarely produced assets for web, email, TikTok, and Facebook simultaneously.

Designed a Content Capture Guide and production framework: shoot brief, location brief, presenter notes, lighting standards, and multi-format delivery specs. Every shoot now produces a full asset library cut for every channel from a single production day.

Platform & Payments

Thirty thousand people entering. Checkout has to hold.

We rebuilt the transaction layer from the ground up — autoscaling infrastructure so peak draw windows don't take the site down, multi-gateway payment processing with full PCI-DSS compliance, and real-time order reconciliation that tracks every entry against every draw.

On top of that, a formal bug bounty programme to create a responsible disclosure channel, and a documented security incident protocol so the team knew exactly what to do if something went wrong — not scrambling in the moment.

The UX side was equally considered: basket design, checkout flow, Instant Win experience variants — all mapped in Figma, tested with real users, then built into the platform.
