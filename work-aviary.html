The Aviary
Floral

Brand identity, photography and website for a new floral studio — from first brief to fully booked in ten weeks.

Client

The Aviary Floral

Engagement

New business launch

Services

Brand Identity, Photography, Web Design & Build, Content

Duration

10 weeks (launch) + ongoing

The situation

The craft was there from day one. Everything else needed to be built around it.

The Aviary Floral came to us at the best and most difficult moment for any new business: ready to launch, with a genuinely distinctive skill and absolutely no brand to show for it yet.

Founder Rachael had years of experience in high-end event and wedding floristry — but was stepping out on her own for the first time. The challenge wasn't the quality of the work. It was that nobody knew it existed, and there was no visual identity in place to make a first impression worth making.

In a market where a floral studio's Instagram presence can determine whether it's taken seriously or not, arriving without a name, a mark, photography, or a website would have meant starting at a significant disadvantage. We had ten weeks from brief to launch.

"Ready to launch with something genuinely beautiful — and nothing to show a potential client yet."

The photography Astutely created

A complete visual library — built to last far beyond the launch.

We art-directed and shot a two-day launch campaign: studio arrangements, in-environment installations, lifestyle context, and process documentation. The resulting library gave The Aviary an immediate social presence and enough depth to populate the site, editorial pitches, and press materials from day one.

Why we were brought in

Launching without a brand is the hardest way to start.

A new business in a visually competitive market has a very narrow window to make an impression. Without a coherent identity and strong photography, word of mouth can only carry things so far — and in floristry, the first image a potential client sees often determines whether they enquire at all.

Rachael came to us with the skill, the eye, and the drive. What she needed was a professional foundation built fast enough to meet the moment.

No brand identity. No name treatment, no mark, no colour system, no typography. Nothing to put on a business card, a website, or a social profile.

No photography. The work was stunning in person, but there was no visual record of it — and in floristry, a portfolio is everything.

No web presence. No website, no searchability, no way for interested clients to find out more or make an enquiry at any hour of the day.

Premium positioning at risk. The quality of the work deserved a premium price point — but without a brand to match it, that positioning would be hard to hold.

Ten weeks to launch. There was a hard deadline. Everything — name, identity, shoot, site — had to be ready at the same time.

What we did

Brand, lens, and site — built together so everything speaks the same language from day one.

01

Brand identity

No mark, no palette, no visual language. Without a brand, every piece of marketing, every social post, and every client touchpoint would feel disconnected — undermining the premium positioning Rachael had earned through her craft.

A refined identity built around restraint and natural form. The logotype draws on botanical illustration — unhurried, precise, quietly distinctive. A tight palette of warm off-whites, deep teals, and earthy mauve tones that reads as considered rather than corporate. The system works as well on a business card as it does on a full-page editorial spread.

02

Photography & launch content

Without a portfolio, any new client enquiry had to be won purely on reputation and referral — a ceiling that would cap growth from the start. In floristry, the photograph is the product before the product exists.

A full two-day launch photography campaign — studio arrangements, in-environment lifestyle work, installation shots, and process documentation — art-directed to tell the story of making, not just the finished piece. The resulting library gave The Aviary an immediate social presence and enough depth for editorial pitches and press materials from day one of trading.

03

Website & online presence

No website meant no discoverability. Interested clients had no way to learn more, see the portfolio, understand the services, or make an enquiry outside of a direct referral — which meant opportunities were being lost before they even began.

A portfolio-first website built on Shopify that lets the work lead. Services, approach, and enquiry forms structured to convert browsers into qualified leads. Built to be updated easily by the founder without technical support. Fast, mobile-first, and designed for the moment a potential wedding or event client is comparing their options.

The full picture

Outside of the arrangements themselves, there was very little The Aviary had when they came to us. We handed them back a name, a mark, a colour system, two days of photography, a full website, and a social presence — all coherent, all ready, all on time.

Faux flower arrangements

Every arrangement photographed to sell — not just to catalogue.

The product photography we created gave each arrangement its own story — styled, lit, and shot to sit comfortably in a luxury home. Not product shots, but desire shots.

Dried flower collections

From minimal to wild — a full palette of dried arrangements.

Each dried collection photographed in the same consistent style — clean, warm, with the textures and depth of the flowers doing the work.

Door wreaths

Statement pieces — shot to be as beautiful as the real thing.

Wreaths are a significant part of The Aviary's range and some of the first items to sell seasonally. The photography gives each one the staging it deserves.

Weddings & events

Bespoke event work needs to be sold before it exists.

A portfolio that gives confidence to a couple planning a wedding or a venue commissioning a centrepiece installation. We built that portfolio from scratch — and the photography has done the work every time someone new visits the site.

The wedding shoot gave The Aviary an immediate case study for every enquiry in the event category: proof of scale, proof of aesthetic, proof that the studio can deliver at the level clients in that market expect.
