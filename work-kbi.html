Kickboxing
Ireland

A new visual identity and digital presence for Ireland's national kickboxing governing body — 12,000 members, one mark.

Client

Kickboxing Ireland

Engagement

Governing body rebrand

Services

Brand Identity, Visual System, Website, Digital Assets

Sector

Sport & Community

The situation

Ireland's fourth-ranked sport in the world — with a brand that didn't say so.

Kickboxing Ireland is the national governing body for kickboxing across the island. Over 12,000 members. More than 275 qualified coaches. Ireland ranked fourth in the world under WAKO. A sport that competes at European and World Championship level every year.

But the visual identity and digital presence didn't communicate any of that. The organisation had outgrown its look — and as the sport continued to grow, that gap between substance and presentation was becoming harder to ignore.

KBI needed a brand that could represent them at every level: on a championship floor in Portugal or Abu Dhabi, in a community sports hall in Tallaght, on a parent's phone screen when they're searching for their child's first club.

"12,000 members, world-class athletes, and a brand that wasn't keeping pace with any of it."

The people behind the brand

A sport that runs on coaches, juniors, and community.

KBI's membership spans ages from eight to eighty. Club members, elite squad athletes, national coaches, parents, and funders — all needing to connect with the same organisation through the same brand.

Why we were brought in

A governing body needs to look like one.

A national sports governing body has an unusually wide range of people it needs to speak to at once: elite athletes preparing for World Championships, ten-year-olds training for their first competition, parents choosing a first club, coaches seeking development pathways, and funders evaluating whether to invest in the sport.

The brand has to hold credibility with all of them — and the existing identity wasn't doing that job.

Brand didn't reflect the scale. Ireland ranked 4th in the world. 12,000 members. The visual identity didn't communicate any of that ambition or standing.

Multiple audiences, one front door. Athletes, parents, coaches, clubs, and funders all land on the same website and need to find very different things very quickly.

No consistent digital system. Social media assets, event graphics, and squad communications all looked different. No shared visual language across the organisation.

Website built for admin, not for people. Hard to navigate, not mobile-optimised, and didn't reflect the energy or professionalism of the sport.

International competition, local recruitment. KBI needed one brand that could represent Ireland on a world stage and still feel relevant to a teenager in a community sports hall.

What we did

One mark that works everywhere — the gym wall, the world championship floor, the mobile screen.

01

Brand identity & visual system

The existing brand communicated neither the scale of the organisation nor the energy of the sport. It looked like an administrative body — not a governing body with world-class athletes and a community of 12,000 people behind it.

A dynamic new identity built on the KBI mark — bold, angular, kinetic. The signature lime green creates instant recognition and communicates energy without losing credibility. A complete visual system: typography, colour, iconography, and brand voice that works from a gym wall vinyl to a TV broadcast graphic. Built to be used consistently by clubs and coaches across the island.

02

Website & digital presence

The website was built around how the organisation worked internally, not around how people needed to use it. Finding a local club, registering as a member, understanding coaching pathways — all required far more effort than they should.

A rebuilt website organised around audience — athlete, parent, coach, club, funder — with clear navigation paths for each. Club finder integrated with the membership system. Events and news structured for mobile-first browsing. Built on WordPress with a content management system the KBI team can manage without technical support.

03

Digital templates & social assets

Social media content across KBI was inconsistent — clubs, coaches, and the national body all posting in different styles with no shared visual language. Events and squads were promoted without a recognisable system holding it together.

A full suite of branded social media templates, event graphics, squad announcement assets, and championship promotional materials — all built on the new identity system and ready to use. Clubs and coaches can produce on-brand content without design resource. The KBI brand now reads consistently from the national team's Instagram to a local club's Facebook post.

The identity

Built to be bold — and to hold up anywhere the sport appears.

The KBI mark needed to work at thumbnail size on a mobile app and at 3 metres tall on a championship backdrop. The lime green became the signature: distinctive, energetic, unmistakably KBI.
