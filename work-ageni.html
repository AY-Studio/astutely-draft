The Situation

Great content. Not enough people finding it.

Age NI serves hundreds of thousands of older people across Northern Ireland — free advice, direct care, a helpline that's often the first call someone makes when things get hard. The information on their website is genuinely valuable. The challenge was making sure the people who needed it could actually find it.

They came to us with an internal team already in place — people who cared about the work and knew the audience deeply. What they needed was external expertise to help them understand how the site was performing in search, where the gaps were, and how to start closing them.

On top of the SEO work, there was a UX question: a site serving older users — many of whom may be less confident with technology — needed to be genuinely easy to use, not just technically accessible.

"AY transformed our digital strategy with a cost-effective solution, saving us money and reducing maintenance."

Niall Browne — Marketing Director, Age NI
The challenges

Where the site was leaving people behind.

The problems weren't about content — Age NI had plenty of that. They were about visibility, structure, and making the experience work for the people who needed it most.

  • SEO scores not reflecting the quality of the content. Pages covering important topics — benefits advice, care guidance, helpline services — weren't ranking where they should. The content was good; the technical and structural foundations were holding it back.

  • Technical SEO issues across the site. Missing or inconsistent meta data, heading hierarchy issues, page speed gaps, and structured data that hadn't been set up to help search engines understand the content.

  • Navigation not built around how older users actually browse. The site structure made sense from an organisational perspective, but not from a user one — especially for visitors who might be less confident online, arriving with an urgent question and needing a clear route to an answer.

  • An internal team that needed support, not replacement. Age NI had capable people. The goal wasn't to take over — it was to work alongside them, build their understanding of what was driving performance, and give them the tools to keep improving after the engagement.

What we did

Support the team.
Improve what matters.

SEO Audit & Strategy

No clear picture of where the site was underperforming, which pages mattered most, or how the site was structured from a search perspective.

Carried out a full technical and content SEO audit — crawl analysis, keyword mapping to existing content, priority scoring, and a clear implementation roadmap the team could work from. Focus was on pages that drove the most real-world impact: helpline, benefits advice, care services.

Technical SEO Implementation

Meta titles and descriptions were missing or generic across large sections of the site. Heading structure was inconsistent. Page speed and structured data hadn't been configured.

Worked directly with the Age NI team to implement improvements across the site — writing meta data for priority pages, correcting heading hierarchies, configuring structured data for key service content, and addressing the technical issues surfaced in the audit.

UX & Usability Improvements

Navigation and content structure made sense organisationally, but not always for a user arriving from search with a specific, often urgent, need — particularly for an older audience less familiar with complex site structures.

Reviewed the site from the user's perspective, identifying friction points in key journeys — finding the helpline, accessing benefits information, navigating to care services. Made targeted improvements to labelling, link structure, and page layout to reduce the number of steps between arrival and answer.

Who it's for

Older people across Northern Ireland. Using the website to find advice, access services, and connect with support that makes a real difference.

technical SEO audit, keyword mapping, meta data implementation, structured data, heading structure, page speed, navigation improvements — and practical support for a team who already cared deeply about the people they served.
